The Science Behind Readability Formulas

Flesch Reading Ease

Developed by Rudolf Flesch in 1948, this is one of the oldest and most validated readability formulas. It measures how easy a text is to understand based on sentence length and syllable count.

Formula:
206.835 - (1.015 × ASL) - (84.6 × ASW)

Where ASL = average sentence length, ASW = average syllables per word

Flesch-Kincaid Grade Level

Adapted from Flesch's work for the U.S. Navy, this formula translates readability scores into U.S. grade levels, making it easier to match content to audience education levels.

Formula:
(0.39 × ASL) + (11.8 × ASW) - 15.59

Gunning Fog Index

Developed by Robert Gunning, this index specifically measures text complexity by counting complex words (those with three or more syllables).

Formula:
0.4 × (ASL + Percentage of Complex Words)

Practical Tips to Improve Readability

1. Shorten Your Sentences

Aim for an average of 15-20 words per sentence. Break complex ideas into multiple sentences to reduce cognitive load.

2. Simplify Vocabulary

Replace Latinate words with Anglo-Saxon equivalents: "utilize" becomes "use," "approximately" becomes "about."

3. Use Active Voice

Active constructions are typically 15-20% easier to process than passive voice.

4. Implement Strategic Formatting

Use headings, bullet points, and short paragraphs to create visual breathing space.
